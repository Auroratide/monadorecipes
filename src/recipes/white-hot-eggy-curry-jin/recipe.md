# White-Hot Eggy Curry

A dry Japanese-style curry that uses poultry as a base.

* Type: Meat
* Source: Jin's Home Cooking
* Prep: 20 minutes
* Time: 20 minutes
* Rarity: 1

## Ingredients

| Ingredient           | Quantity       | Rarity | Type      |
| -------------------- | -------------- | ------ | --------- |
| Ground Turkey        | 1 pound        | 2      | Meat      |
| Onion (diced)        | 1              | 1      | Vegetable |
| Garlic (minced)      | 2 cloves       | 1      | Vegetable |
| Green Bell Pepper (minced) | 1        | 1      | Vegetable |
| Carrot (minced)      | 2              | 1      | Vegetable |
| Chicken Broth        | 1/3 cup        | 1      | Meat      |
| Worcestershire Sauce | 3 tablespoons  | 3      | Spice     |
| Curry Powder         | 1 1/2 tablespoons | 2   | Spice     |
| Soy Sauce            | 1 tablespoon   | 1      | Spice     |
| Ketchup              | 2 tablespoons  | 1      | Vegetable |
| Salt and Pepper      | -              | 1      | Spice     |
| Oil                  | 2 tablespoons  | 1      | Oil       |
| Eggs (fried)         | 4              | 1      | Meat      |
| Rice                 | -              | 1      | Grain     |

## Directions

1. Start cooking the rice in your favorite rice cooker.
2. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the onion to it and saute for 5 minutes, until translucent. [Oil: 1 tablespoon, Onion (diced): 1]
3. Add the ground turkey, and season with salt and pepper. Stir to combine. [Ground Turkey: 1 pound, Salt and Pepper: -]
4. Add the minced garlic, and stir until the meat is browned. [Garlic (minced): 2 cloves]
5. Add carrot and bell pepper and mix, then cover the pan with a lid and lower the heat to medium-low. Cook for about 3 minutes. [Carrot (minced): 2, Green Bell Pepper (minced): 1]
6. Add the curry powder and mix well. Then, add the rest of the seasonings: chicken broth, worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, and ketchup. [Curry Powder: 1 1/2 tablespoons, Chicken Broth: 1/3 cup, Worcestershire Sauce: 3 tablespoons, Soy Sauce: 1 tablespoon, Ketchup: 2 tablespoons]
7. Cover the pan again and cook over low heat for 5 minutes.
8. While the curry is cooking, you can fry the eggs. A good way to fry the eggs is to heat 1 tablespoon of oil over high heat. Once hot, reduce the heat to medium and crack the egg(s) into the pan, seasoned with a pinch of salt. Fry only on one side, using a spoon to scoop some hot oil onto the top of the egg. When the whites are set (about 2 minutes), scoop the egg out and set aside. [Eggs: 4, Oil: 1 tablespoon]
9. Serve by putting the curry atop a bed of rice, then placing a fried egg on top of the curry!

## Interpretation

* Rhogul Birdmeat: 1
* Meaty Carrot: 1
* Deviled Onion: 1
* Creeping Starpepper: 1
* Barbed Tomato: 1

Jin's version is related to, but significantly different from, [Crossette's curry](/recipes/white-hot-eggy-curry). Like Crossette's, Jin's curry is Japanese-inspired, but since his version lacks potatos and uses birdmeat, we went with a dry curry where the meat is the focus.

Normally, Japanese dry curry is made with pork or beef, but in our recipe we use ground turkey to represent the Rhoguls. The rest of the ingredients are all naturally part of dry curry (carrots, onions, bell peppers for the starpepper, and ketchup for the tomato), and we top it with a fried egg so the curry becomes "eggy".
